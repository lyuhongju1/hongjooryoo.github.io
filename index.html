As a researcher at UC Berkeley, I am primarily dedicated to advancing our understanding of high-energy particle experimentation and lattice field theories. My academic journey is rooted in the pursuit of knowledge across multiple disciplines, including physics, applied mathematics, philosophy, and cognitive science.

My current research focuses on computational studies within the Berkeley Nuclear Theory Group, N3AS, and experimental work with the Orebi Gann Group at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. I was also involved in theoretical physics, examining high-energy phenomena near black holes for my Honors Thesis. In addition, my honors research in Philosophy is centered around incorporating context-dependence within the Kairetic account of explanation. Each project aims to deepen our grasp of quantum phenomena and the fundamental principles governing the universe.
