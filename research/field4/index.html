Philosophy of Explanation Research

In my philosophy work, I have developed the context-dependent mapping (fc mapping) framework as an extension to Strevens' Kairetic account of scientific explanation and Railton's DNP account for simple probabilities in an attempt to elucidate how varying experimental or contextual conditions lead to distinct manifestations of phenomena, particularly in quantum mechanics and general relativity. This approach allows for a nuanced understanding and accommodation of complementarities in these fields, emphasizing the influence of specific contexts on observational outcomes. Looking forward, I plan to build upon this framework to explore how similar mapping principles can be applied in other domains, such as moral theories and aesthetics, aiming to unify disparate theoretical frameworks and enhance our understanding of complex, context-sensitive phenomena across disciplines.
